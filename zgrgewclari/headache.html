ClariHealth: Headache

A headache is a common pain experience that can be in any area in your head. In fact, it's so common that everybody has at least experienced it once in their lifetime. They also affect kids and adults of both genders. There are many types of headaches but the most common type of headache is called Tension headache. Tension basically means a tightness in a place, so Tension headache is just a type of headache where the muscle in your shoulder, jaw, scalp, and neck get tight and stretched.
Some common causes of headache are: Stress Anxiety depression You can also get a headache if you don't sleep enough, working too much, dehydration, missing meals, or drinking alcohol.

During the experience,you might feel a dull ache, a sensation of pressure, or like a tight band is wrapped around your head. It's usually mild and temporary. It also lasts from as little as 30 minutes up to a week, and they typically pass with rest and self-care.

Some of the things you can do to at least remove some of the pain is staying hydrated. It is very important to drink water often. This can help with removing some of the pain from your head. Another way is to manage your stress. Take deep breaths when your stress levels trigger. This way, you can stay more calm and get less headaches. There are also experiences that if you felt or dealt with any of them. I would recommend seeking a healthcare professional. Here are signs that a healthcare professional is recommended in these cases: A sudden, new and severe headache. Headache with a fever, shortness of breath, stiff neck or rash. Headaches that occur after a head injury or accident. Getting a new type of headache after age 55. Seek medical care immediately if you notice any of these neurological symptoms: Weakness. Dizziness. Sudden loss of balance or falling. Numbness or tingling. Vision changes (blurry vision, double vision or blind spots).

In conclusion, a headache is very common and very treatable with at home care or with a simple doctor or pharmacist visit.These are common and usually resolve on their own with rest and hydration. It is always ok to talk to a healthcare professional if you're unsure about something.
